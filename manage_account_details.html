Manage account details

You can work with your advisor to make changes to your account, or simply log in to the Schwab Alliance website to make the changes yourself. Once you’re online, you can review statements, sign up for convenient electronic document delivery, and keep track of your investments on the go with our mobile apps. And when you need to move money, the fastest way is to simply contact your advisor.

Get started

It’s easy to get started with a new Schwab account. Your advisor will help you open the account and transfer assets. Once it is open, you can set up online access to your account in a few simple steps— and download the Schwab Mobile app to keep up with your account anywhere, anytime. Below you’ll find simple explanations of each of these activities.

Sign up for eDelivery

eDelivery gives you convenient, paperless access to up to 10 years of important account information through the security of Schwab Alliance. Just follow the simple steps below. If you don’t yet have online access to your account, you can sign up for access and eDelivery in one simple process.

Talk to your advisor

Once your advisor initiates the process, you’ll receive an invitation email. Follow the simple instructions to select the eDelivery options that are right for you.

- OR -

1

Log in

If you prefer to sign up for eDelivery yourself, log in to Schwab Alliance, navigate to the Service tab, and click on Alert Preferences. Choose Paperless Services.

2

Choose options

Click on the arrow next to each document type you would like to receive electronically. You can enroll all of your accounts in eDelivery or choose individual accounts.

Make account changes

The easiest way to make changes to your account is by contacting your advisor, who can walk you through the process and provide any forms you may need to sign. You can also log in to Schwab Alliance to change account details at any time. Once you’ve signed up for online access, follow the steps below to make the necessary changes.

  • Change address
  • Add someone to your account
  • Standing letter of authorization
  • Power of attorney

Talk to your advisor

The easiest way to change your address is to contact your advisor, who can complete the necessary forms and send them for your signature or initiate an electronic request for you to approve on Schwab Alliance or your mobile device.

- OR -

1

Log in

If you prefer to make address changes yourself, log in to Schwab Alliance, navigate to the Service tab, and click on My Profile.

2

Update physical address

Click the Mailing Address tab to update your physical address. For your security, Schwab allows only U.S. mailing addresses to be registered through Schwab Alliance; you cannot enter a PO box. Note that you can also change your phone number.

3

Update email address

Click the Email Address tab to update your primary and other email addresses.

Moving money

There are a number of options for moving money into and out of your account. Your advisor will help you transfer funds between Schwab accounts or to outside accounts. For recurring transfers, your advisor can help you set up a Schwab MoneyLink®. You can also deposit checks through your mobile device and request disbursement checks through your advisor. We take our role as custodian seriously. When you ask your advisor to move money or assets on your behalf, we’ll often require your authorization, even if you have made a similar request before. You may be able to set up a standing letter of authorization for some transactions. Simply choose the type of change you’d like to make from the options below and follow the instructions. For more information, see our FAQ.

  • Transfer a wire
  • Journal between Schwab accounts
  • Request a check
  • Deposit a check
  • Set up a Schwab MoneyLink®

Wires are one way of quickly transferring money from a Schwab account to an account at another institution. Your advisor can initiate wires electronically or using paper and fax. For your safety and security, Schwab requires that your advisor or Schwab must verify all wire requests. This may mean that you receive a phone call for confirmation.

Understand your statements

Schwab keeps you up-to-date on the status of your investments through your account statement.

  • You can receive your statements by mail or use eDelivery to receive them electronically.
  • You will also find up to 10 years of statements and other important documents online through Schwab Alliance.
  • When reviewing your statements, pay special attention to the “Change in Account Value” section, where you’ll find key data points that affect your account’s value, including:
    • Deposits and withdrawals
    • Investments purchased and sold
    • Fees from the reporting period covered by the statement
    • Your statement also lists your current holdings and recent account transactions
